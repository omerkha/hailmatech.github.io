What are CSCS Cards?

A CSCS Card shows that you have the skills required to work well in your trade and that you have the Health and Safety knowledge needed to work safely. CSCS Cards are graded by a colour system, the CSCS Card you qualify for depends on your level of training, experience and qualifications. Your CSCS Card will be specific to your trade and will also contain relevant information about you on its computer chip so that the information can be checked on site. Most principal contractors and major house builders require construction workers on their sites to hold a valid CSCS card.

Do I need a CSCS Card?

To work on a construction site there is no legal obligation to have a card before you work on site. However, all the major contractors who are members of UKCG have agreed that workers on their sites need to have a CSCS card. The law requires that those participating in construction work must be competent.

Do I need qualifications to apply for a CSCS Card?

To apply for a CSCS card, you must have the appropriate construction related qualifications and training. In most cases, you must have passed the CITB Health, safety and environment test within the previous two years.  If you have achieved a NVQ or a SVQ within the last two years and are applying for a CSCS card for the occupation the NVQ or SVQ relates to, you may not have to pass a CITB Health, Safety and Environment Test.

Overseas Qualifications

The National Academic Recognition Information Centre for the United Kingdom (UK NARIC) provide a service to compare foreign qualifications with their UK equivalent. UK NARIC will issue a Statement of Comparability if a non-UK qualification is acceptable for UK Registration.

How long does the CSCS card last for?

How long your CSCS card lasts depends on the card you’re applying for, but most cards last for five years. When you receive your card it will show an expiry date.

How long does the card take to arrive?

Usually delivery should be within 30 working days.

Which Type of CSCS Card Do I Need?

Red CSCS Card There are 6 types of Red CSCS Card – Trainee (Craft & Operative), Trainee (TSM), Experienced Worker, Experienced TSM, Provisional and Apprentice. Red CSCS Cards are usually issued while workers are in further education or are working towards an NVQ/SVQ.

Green (Labourer) CSCS Card The Green CSCS Card is issued to labourers. This CSCS Card usually requires a L1 Award in Health & Safety in a Construction Environment or SSP Health & Safety Awareness Course. Also you need to pass the Health, Safety & Environment Operative test.

Blue CSCS Card The Blue CSCS Card requires a Level 2 NVQ/SVQ or an City & Guilds Craft certificate. You must also pass the Health, Safety & Environment Test which is relevant to your trade to get this CSCS Card.

Gold CSCS Card To get a Gold CSCS Card you must have an indentured apprenticeship or an NVQ/SVQ Level 3. There are both Skilled worker and Supervisor Gold CSCS Cards, you must pass either the Health, Safety & Environment Test relevant to your trade or the Supervisor test.

Black CSCS Card The Black CSCS Card is for managers. Achievement of a Construction related NVQ/SVQ levels 5, 6 or 7 in the relevant construction management level qualification or Holding a pre-existing NVQ level 4 in construction management and the successful completion of a Health, safety and environment test for managers and professionals.

Professionally Qualified Person This card is available to competence assessed members of CSCS approved Professional Bodies. You Must also pass the Health, safety and environment test for managers and professionals.

Academically Qualified Person This card is available to people who have completed certain construction related degrees, HNDs, HNCs, CIOB Certificates and NEBOSH diplomas. You Must also pass the Health, safety and environment test for managers and professionals.

Construction Site Visitor This card is available to people who frequently visit a construction site, but have no construction specific skill. You must pass the CITB Operatives Health, Safety and Environment Test.
